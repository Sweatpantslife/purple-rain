> **Phase 2→3 bridge document.** One entry per scene — all **58** (A0: 8, A1: 9, A2: 14, A3: 16, A4: 11) — tying the narrative beats of [`../storyboard/SCENES.md`](../storyboard/SCENES.md) to the player experience defined in [`GAMEPLAY.md`](GAMEPLAY.md). Canon priority: [`../narrative/STORY.md`](../narrative/STORY.md) / [`../narrative/MYSTERY_LOGIC.md`](../narrative/MYSTERY_LOGIC.md) / [`../narrative/TIMELINE.md`](../narrative/TIMELINE.md), then the storyboard, then GAMEPLAY.md, then this document. Nothing here moves a clue, a reveal, a valve, or a teaching beat; any conflict resolves upward.
>
> **Spoiler policy:** total spoilers. Playtesters and blind contractors should not read this document.

## How to read an entry

- **Pacing mix** — rough experiential share of scene time across seven buckets: *action / stealth / investigation / puzzle / dialogue / traversal / cutscene*. Percentages are design targets, not stopwatch law.
- **Player verbs** — the GAMEPLAY.md systems exercised, by their canonical names (**Field Notebook**, **Evidence Board**, **Heat**, etc.), with puzzle-taxonomy tags (*Hydraulic Routing*, *Vestry Cipher*, …) where a puzzle runs.
- **What the player learns** — the information payload: clue plants/payoffs (`C-##`), red herrings planted or debunked (`RH-##`), reveals (`R0`–`R5`). Placement is canonical (MYSTERY_LOGIC.md §1–2, §5); do not move.
- **Emotional target** — the feeling the scene must land, and the design levers that produce it.
- **Length target** — rough minutes and intensity label (**calm / tense / spike**). Two intensity axes are tracked in the act charts (§Pacing): **M** = mechanical demand on the player's hands, **E** = emotional load. A grief cutscene is M-calm and E-spike; the two must not be conflated in tuning.

Act time budgets follow GAMEPLAY.md §5: Act 0 ≈ 45–60 min · Act I ≈ 2 h · Act II ≈ 4 h · Act III ≈ 4 h · Act IV ≈ 3 h. Per-scene minutes below sum to the critical path; **Favor Board** content fills the remainder in Acts II–III.

# ACT 0 — GRACE (8 scenes, ≈ 53 min critical path)

*The loop taught in civilian trim. No fail states, no Heat UI. Every scene is a loaded gun the player must enjoy holding.*

### A0-S01 — "Anniversary Morning"
- **Pacing mix:** investigation 40 / traversal 25 / dialogue 20 / cutscene 15
- **Player verbs:** **Field Notebook** tutorialized as habit (examine, jot, flag); movement and interaction basics; the **Core Loop**'s INVESTIGATE phase in domestic miniature.
- **What the player learns:** **C-01** planted (Mara's "three more sick kids from the Flats" — pure domestic color); **C-32** seeded (Gabriel Vale's shelf photograph, examinable — journal note: Works pipefitter, bladder cancer, 2007, "never connected to anything"). The notebook shelf (41 spines) establishes the habit the warrant will later know too well.
- **Emotional target:** unguarded domestic warmth — the player must want this life. Levers: rain-as-comfort audio, tactile morning props (kettle, lanyard, anniversary card), zero UI chrome, banter written to be worn smooth.
- **Length target:** ~8 min — calm.

### A0-S02 — "The City in the Rain"

# Monotony risks — identical adjacent pacing mixes

Scan of all 57 adjacent pairs for identical mixes. Three findings, in severity order:

### RISK 1 — A3-S07 → A3-S08 → A3-S09: three consecutive 100%-cutscene scenes (~22 min without meaningful input)
The reversal, the low point, and the funeral. The run is **partly deliberate** — canon withholds interactivity at A3-S08 explicitly ("the scene's design withholds music, mercy, and interactivity alike") and the M-flatline under the E-maximum is the act's design signature. But three identical mixes in a row is the longest input drought in the game outside credits, and it lands right before the game's most athletic scene (A3-S10), risking a cold-hands spike. Mitigations that respect canon:
- **A3-S07:** keep the scene authored, but let the aftermath play on the **Evidence Board** — a short interactive coda in which the player watches the debunk *correct their own handwriting* (wrong strings contradicted, C-25's card re-graded under scrutiny). This is existing Evidence Board doctrine (GAMEPLAY.md §2.3.5), adds ~90 seconds of input, changes no clue placement.
