# PURPLE RAIN — Timeline (v1.0)

> **True chronology vs. what the player sees.** Companion to [`STORY.md`](STORY.md) and [`MYSTERY_LOGIC.md`](MYSTERY_LOGIC.md). Three sections:
> §1 — the true chronology of the conspiracy (event IDs `T-<year><letter>`);
> §2 — the game-present calendar (Acts 0–IV, day by day);
> §3 — the frame-up construction schedule (month by month);
> §4 — the discovery map: the order in which the *player* learns the truth, versus when it happened.
>
> **Dating convention:** the game never displays calendar years on screen (dates render as "the 14th," "seven years ago"). Internally, all docs use the anchor **Year 0 = the game present**, with absolute years below for authoring convenience. Present = 2026. If production shifts the anchor, shift every year below uniformly; all relative spans are load-bearing, absolute years are not.

## §2 Game-Present Calendar (Acts 0–IV)

Anchors: **Day 0** = the raid (A0-S08). The whole game spans ≈ 31 weeks, April → early November, ending on ratification day in Storm Vesper — deliberately: the story starts in spring rain and ends in the year's worst weather.

| Span | Act | Key fixed dates |
|------|-----|-----------------|
| Day −3 … Day 0 | **Act 0 — Grace** | Day −3 file/email tripwire; Day −3 eve anniversary dinner; Day 0 dawn raid |
| Day 0 … Day 18 | **Act I — The Fall** | Day 1 arraignment/charge sheet; Day 4 plea offer; Day 6 bail (Fidelis); Day 9 Mara leaves; Day 14 Skerry surfaces; Day 17 ROOK9's first move; Day 18 the run → Undercroft |
| Day 18 … ~Week 14 | **Act II — Hell** | Weeks 3–6 Lantern/jobs/Nine/Kray; Week 7 Duval; Week 8 archive heist (**midpoint**) + R1; Week 9 compromise order + Lantern near-miss; Week 10 St. Alban's cipher; Week 11 Nightingale hall; Week 12 Skerry murdered & authored onto Adam; Week 13 Theo wire-trap & confession; Week 14 H.G. print mailed to Whitlock |

## §3 The Frame: Construction Schedule (T-2025d–h expanded)

Every component pre-dates Day 0 by months — this table is the "mechanical plausibility" audit for the frame-up, and the source of its datable flaws.

| Month | Action | Operator | Residue (the flaw it leaves) |
|-------|--------|----------|------------------------------|
| 2025 Oct | "Vale problem" memo adopted; frame spec drafted (targets, allocution dates, notebook seizure list) | Cordell → Quorum → Marr | Warrant over-specificity (C-10); allocution window (C-11) |
| 2025 Oct–Nov | Offshore structure stood up; **backdated** transaction history assembled 2019–2025 | Skerry (broker), Aegis finance | One 2023-dated wire uses a 2024-only message format (**C-27**) |
| 2025 Nov | Theo Vale's gambling debts purchased via shell; "errand" recruitment begins | Aegis (cutout) | Debt-purchase paper trail; Theo's behavioral collapse (A0-S06) |

| 2025 Dec–2026 Feb | "**Violet Marsh**" persona built: aged social accounts, second phone provisioned, hotel charges seeded; signature exemplar for the joint account **traced from the Vales' mortgage** | Aegis ops; named by Cordell | Phone provisioning predates its own "purchase receipt"; Mara's backwards-E signature habit not known to forgers (A3-S15); the name itself (C-35) |
| 2026 Jan | Skerry retained and scripted; grand-jury package pre-assembled for the DA's office | Marr; fed to Hale's office via "task force" | Skerry's private insurance tapes (A4-S09); Hale's case "building itself" (C-13) |
| 2026 Feb | Records QA intercept re-verified; archive-annex choreography drafted (staged box, purge schedule, compromise-order template) *in case the fugitive track is needed* | Cordell's office; Marr | The pre-drafted order and choreography memo (C-17 payoff, A4-S02) |
| 2026 Mar | CLEARWATER notices go out (public, scheduled — not theirs); frame put on hair-trigger | — | C-03 |
| 2026 Apr (Day −3) | Adam's QA email trips the wire; "go" | Cordell (reads), Marr (executes) | The bounce (C-09); the raid's impossible readiness (72 hours from email to a 46-count indictment) — noted aloud by the defense (C-13) |

**Contingency ladder (as drafted by Cordell, T-2025c):** Plan A — plea + allocution (the record poisoned by its own author, 30-month sentence, done). Plan B — refusal → engineered bail (C-12) → provoked flight (flight = public confession; fugitive available for choreography and authored crimes). Plan C — (added Week 12 over Cordell's objection) termination, with posthumous confession package. Plan D — (never drafted; the hole in the ladder) *what if he stops using channels we can see?* There is no Plan D. The Undercroft is Plan D's absence. That is the design thesis in operational form.

**Authoring rule:** the player must always learn *effects before causes* (noir order), and every cause, once revealed, must be checkable against effects the player personally experienced. The discovery map above is the canonical audit of that rule; Phase 2 quest design must not reorder rows 5, 7, 9, 11 (the reveal spine) without updating STORY.md §4.7 and MYSTERY_LOGIC.md §5.
