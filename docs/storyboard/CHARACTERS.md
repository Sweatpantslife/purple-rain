## CH-adam / Adam Vale, 38
**Aliases / labels:** "the author of the largest environmental-data fraud in state history" (Hale's press line); "the fugitive engineer"; "the engineer" (Undercroft usage, esp. Kray); wanted-bulletin mugshot face of the whole back half of the game.

### Appearance
Marsh stock made lean by stairs and fieldwork: 5'11", wiry-strong rather than gym-built, the forearms of a man who has hauled sampling rigs up embankments for fifteen years. Dark brown hair cut short and practical, going unmanaged and longer through Acts II–III; grey arrives at the temples across the game — visibly, deliberately, as a costume-of-time device. Pale hazel-grey eyes ("water-colored" is the concept brief) that do the character's acting: he watches moving water the way other people watch faces. A weathered, likable, unremarkable face — the design requirement is that he is *plausible* on both a staff badge and a wanted poster. Hands always slightly scarred and nicked; a habit of touching surfaces (walls, railings, pipe runs) to feel for vibration.

**Costume phases (artist brief):**
- **Grace (A0):** MWA softshell jacket over flannel, worn hiking boots, laminated badge on a lanyard, field notebook in the left chest pocket — always the left. Wedding ring.
- **The Fall (A1):** the same clothes decaying — suit for court that doesn't fit the situation, then the softshell again with the logo obscured.
- **Hell (A2):** Undercroft layers — oilskin coat gone grey-green at the hems from tunnel water, waders, fingerless gloves, notebook #42 now in a waxed pouch. Beard. The badge lanyard is gone; the ring stays (until the artist team decides otherwise — see Editorial notes).
- **Purgatory/Reckoning (A3–A4):** the layers refined into competence — a dark workman's silhouette that can pass at a gala with Nine's papers and Kray's rag-trade suit (A3-S03) or vanish into a gravedigger's crew (A3-S09). Final look A4-S06: harnessed, soaked, headlamp, the notebook in a chest dry-bag like a relic.

### Personality & voice
Methodical, gentle, a little rigid; allergic to shortcuts; the person colleagues call when the numbers feel wrong. Not hero-shaped — his courage is procedural, a refusal to sign rather than a willingness to fight. Dry, self-deprecating humor that thins under pressure and returns, harder, in the Undercroft. His identity rests on one load-bearing belief — *systems work; findings get filed; the record is real* — and the game is the demolition and honest rebuilding of that belief. Speech pattern: precise, unhurried, engineer's syntax; he answers rhetorical questions literally; under maximum stress he gets *quieter* and more exact, never louder. He narrates the world in measurements when frightened (VO direction: fear = data).

**Sample lines:**
- "The data is real. Test the water." (A1-S02)
- "They didn't frame me to blame me. They framed me to unwrite me." (A2-S08, R1)
- "You are what you sign. Sign this." (A4-S08 — the title-fight line, delivered with the arrest report)

### Role in the story
Protagonist and player character (player-locked except A3-S02 and the broadcast scenes). Senior hydrological engineer, Meridian Water Authority; author of TS-19/44, the 2019 tracer study that proved the Vats→aquifer connection — which makes him not a witness to the crime but its **living evidence**. The frame is aimed backward: discredit the author, void everything he ever signed (R1). His profession is the game's mechanics: reading water — tracer logic, valve networks, pressure maps — is both his superpower and the puzzle grammar. He is also, structurally, the mystery's blind spot generator: his affection for Cordell is the armor the central twist hides behind (MYSTERY_LOGIC.md §6).

### Arc
- **Grace:** a man who trusts the record. Born in Lowmarsh in 1988 — the year of the First Lie, a coincidence Cordell registers bitterly at the end. Son of Gabriel Vale (CH-gabriel), dead of the cluster's bladder cancer in 2007. Married Mara at St. Brigid's under the violet window neither of them ever read (C-33). Forty-one notebooks of a creed his mentor gave him.
- **The Fall:** the record turns on him and he learns it has authors. He refuses the plea — Cordell's one miscalculation and the hinge of the entire plot — and runs.
- **Hell:** stripped of every document-shaped thing, he discovers what remains: competence, memory, the discarded. He becomes capable — locks, lies, stealth — and confronts the fear that the frame was a prophecy (A2-S12: checking whether his face fits the word KILLER).
- **Purgatory:** he chooses what climbs out. Forgives Theo (which dooms him — A2-S13 → A3-S08), canonically takes evidence over blood with Krebs (A3-S10), and makes the true pivot: stops trying to out-document the documenters (A3-S16).
- **Reckoning:** he makes the water testify (A4-S06/S07), hauls his mentor out of the flood rather than letting it have him (A4-S08), skips his own exoneration to read the dismissal order at Theo's grave (A4-S09), and ends by handing a rookie a blank notebook with the creed amended: *"The data doesn't lie. People do. Write it down anyway."* (A4-S10). Heaven is a beginning, not a restoration.

## CH-mara / Mara Vale, 36
**Aliases / labels:** "the wife" in the press cycle; the frame paints her the wronged spouse of "Violet M."'s lover; below, later, simply "the nurse."

### Appearance
Compact, quick, dark-haired — hair habitually pinned up for shift and coming loose by evening. Warm brown eyes with early smile lines and, from Act I on, the specific exhaustion of someone being professionally demolished. Scrubs in clinic teal under a rain shell; sensible shoes gone grey at the toes; a small enamel **clinic pin** (St. Brigid's crest) worn always at the collar — established early and load-bearing (the A3-S09 headstone signal must read at a glance, so the pin must be visually distinctive from A0-S01). Off shift: cardigans, practical color, one good dress for the anniversary (A0-S07). By A3-S15 (night ward) she has a new severity — hair fully back, sleeves rolled, a laminated map-folder always under one arm.

### Personality & voice
Warm, dry-witted, harder-working than anyone upstairs will ever know. Triage-desk temperament: fast sorting, no wasted motion, compassion expressed as competence. Her love language is accuracy — which is why the forged backwards-E signature is, to her, not a clue but an *insult*. Speech pattern: clipped, funny, declarative; she asks exactly one question when one question is enough (A1-S05: "Why was your badge at Records on the 14th?"). VO direction: she never performs grief; she itemizes it.

**Sample lines:**
- "Three more kids on the ward this month from the Flats. Swear that neighborhood is cursed." (A0-S01, C-01 — the whole conspiracy's human cost, offhand, at the breakfast table)
- "This isn't for you. It's for them." (A3-S15)
- "The signature on that account was traced from our mortgage. I sign my E backwards. They didn't know that. You did." (A3-S15 — the marriage un-poisoned in one breath)

### Role in the story
Adam's wife; pediatric nurse at St. Brigid's free clinic. Function in the mystery: **the independent investigator the player mistakes for a betrayer.** Her "sick kids map" — twenty years of triage ledgers charting the Lowmarsh cluster, with the 2014 inflection — is C-01, planted in the first scene and paid off as a clean strand of the convicting lattice (A3-S15, A4-S09). She is the subject of RH-03 (Mara betrayed Adam): the player is meant to half-believe it from A1-S05 until the pin signal (A3-S09) and the full debunk (A3-S15). She didn't turn; she was demolished by professionals — protective order drafted *for* her, clinic funding held hostage — and then she got up and did the work alone.

### Arc
Starts as the marriage's warmth and the theme's quiet witness. Holds through the arrest until the frame's purpose-built marriage-killer detonates (A1-S05) — leaves not out of doubt but out of demolition. Off-screen turn: she re-investigates from the clinic ledgers, cracks the Violet Marsh phone's impossible purchase date and the traced signature herself — *she never needed him to be innocent on faith; she needed data; she is, after all, the person he married.* Signals at Theo's grave (A3-S09), delivers the cluster map for the kids, not for him (A3-S15), stands with him without reconciliation on the chapel steps (A4-S04: "Come back out of this one alive, and we'll see who you are then. I'd like to meet him."), and ends on a first date ten years and one apocalypse after the wedding (A4-S10). Her ending is the theme's proof: not a reset — a beginning.

### Appears in
A0-S01, A0-S07, A0-S08, A1-S04, A1-S05, A3-S09, A3-S15, A4-S04, A4-S10.

## CH-theo / Theo Vale, 29
**Aliases / labels:** "the brother" / "junkie brother of a fraud" (the city's shrug, A3-S08); the frame's unwitting badge vector.

### Appearance
The handsome one, going soft at the edges: Adam's features restrung looser and brighter, mid-length hair, courier's build (bike thighs, bad posture), a wardrobe of borrowed confidence — new phone, fresh sneakers, a jacket a notch flashier than his income. Tells for the artist: betting slips wadded in pockets (A0-S06), a smile that arrives a half-second too fast, hands never still. Across Acts I–II he visibly corrodes — weight loss, grey skin, the over-brightness cracking (his behavioral collapse is a fair-play clue; the model and animation set must support "guilt eating a man alive on screen for two acts"). Dead in A3-S08: staged squalor, needle in his arm — a composition the player must be able to read as *wrong* (Theo faints at needles, C-26).

### Personality & voice
The kid Lowmarsh kept. Funny, generous with money he doesn't have, loves Adam with the abrasive shame of the sibling who never launched. Compulsive deflector: jokes at exactly the wrong moments, over-laughs, presses cash on people as apology. He never asks what the "errands" are for — *because he couldn't afford to. Lowmarsh's oldest skill.* Speech pattern: fast, warm, evasive; sentences that start as jokes and end as confessions.

**Sample lines:**
- "You know me and needles. I'd rather have the flu." (A0-S06 — the joke that detonates six acts later, C-26)
- "I didn't know it was for *you*, Adam, I swear, I didn't *ask*—" (A2-S13, the badge confession)

### Role in the story
Adam's younger brother; gig courier and gambler. Mystery function: **the frame's physical-access vector** — his purchased debts (T-2025e window, TIMELINE §3) bought the loan of Adam's spare MWA badge (C-07), which was cloned to fabricate the Records access logs that convict Adam legally and destroy the marriage (A1-S05). He is also the story's revenge fulcrum: his murder (staged overdose, Marr's cleanup via Krebs, A3-S08) is the low point and powers the A3-S10 morality valve. Design rule preserved from canon: **the game provides no mechanism to harm Theo** (A2-S13).

### Arc
Guilt (A0-S06, visible before the player can name it) → hiding (A1-S06: paid-off slips and a burner the player can find; Adam can't see it, the player can) → confession under the worst circumstances, wearing Marr's wire (A2-S13) → forgiveness he can't accept — Adam's absolution becomes an unpayable debt → a doomed solo penance: taping his confession for Duval (A3-S08) → murdered faster than he can deliver it. Posthumous vindication: his tape and the badge-clone forensics surface from Marr's archive in A4-S09. Adam reads the dismissal order at his grave.

### Appears in
A0-S06, A1-S06, A2-S13, A3-S08 *(dead: CH-theo — body on scene)*. *(His funeral, A3-S09, stages his absence; registry lists him present only as the above.)*

**Appearance (photo/flashback reference only):** big-handed, stooped Lowmarsh laborer in a photograph on the rowhouse shelf — work coat, flat cap, the river behind him; the family resemblance should run Gabriel → Adam (build) and Gabriel → Theo (smile).

## CH-verger / The Verger — Ezra Fenn, 71
**Aliases:** the Verger (universal below; almost no one knows the name Ezra Fenn); formerly sacristan of St. Brigid's, then archivist-verger of the Vestry; "keeper of the Lantern."

### Appearance
Tall and gone thin with age, straight-backed the way lifelong liturgy makes a man — he stands like a candle. Long grey hair tied back, a squared-off beard; deep-set eyes under heavy brows that do "studying you a long moment too long" (A2-S01). Dress is defrocked clerical made practical: black and charcoal layers, a cassock-length oilskin, fingerless gloves, a verger's staff repurposed as a boat-and-wading staff. On his forearm, the **brand-scar** — the Vestry's mark, *given* in exile, not worn in membership (RH-04): the art must let it read first as sinister (half-seen A2-S01) and later as a wound (A2-S10). He smells of candle wax, soup, and river; his hands are an archivist's, immaculate, in a world of mud.

### Personality & voice
Grave, precise, hospitable without warmth-performance; a man who has kept one appointment (the truth) for forty years and expects to be stood up. Speaks in a liturgical register — benedictions, rules of three, ciphered idiom — because it is the language he was raised in and the language the enemy writes in; he is fluent in the city-as-ledger and teaches Adam to read it (the diegetic riddle mentor). Long silences; answers questions with better questions; his rare humor is bone-dry and biblical. VO direction: never whispers, never hurries — the acoustic of a man used to stone rooms.

**Sample lines:**
- "No paper. No questions. No one knows where anyone sleeps." (the Lantern's three rules, A1-S09)
- "The city is the ledger. Learn to read it." (A2-S10)
- "Paper burns. Water remembers." (A3-S16 — the strategic inversion, the story's operative line)

### Role in the story
The game's Virgil: keeper of the Lantern (LOC-lantern), the Undercroft's shelter and the game's hub from Act II. Mystery functions: (1) **the Concordance** — his hand-made duplicate of the Vestry's cipher-minutes 1877–1974, the only record of the Vestry outside the Vestry, the spine of the convicting lattice; (2) **riddle mentor** — he turns the puzzle pillar diegetic (hymn-board ciphers, dedication plaques, the window); (3) **red-herring duty** — RH-04 invites the player to distrust him (the brand, the knowing-too-much, the filtering door riddle) before A2-S10 debunks it: the mark is exile, corroborated by Concordance custody and Bell's parish record.

### Arc
Sacristan → the Vestry's own archivist → 1989: a dying foreman, Uriah Bell (CH-bell), confesses the Vats to him (T-1989) → he raises it inside the Vestry and is destroyed by the playbook's *prototype* — defrocked on manufactured charges, branded, erased (T-1990) → forty years of silence, keeping the door and copying the ledger. His knowledge is legally worthless (hearsay from a dead man, held by a discredited exile) — *he knows it, and it has hollowed him.* His arc completes when Adam builds the provenance lattice that finally lets the Concordance **count** (A4-S02 → A4-S09): silence → witness. Epilogue: declines reinstatement — "I keep the door" — but is seen sweeping St. Alban's drained crypt like a man who might someday hold a service (A4-S10).

### Appears in
A1-S09, A2-S01, A2-S10, A2-S14, A3-S01, A3-S06, A3-S12, A3-S16, A4-S01, A4-S02, A4-S04, A4-S05, A4-S10.

## CH-duval / Iris Duval, 55
**Aliases:** "the crank" (six years of it); formerly the *Meridian Courier*'s finest investigative byline; to the frame's narrative, Adam's "accomplice or dupe" in the retconned 2020 leak.

### Appearance
Once camera-ready, now feral-precise: silver-streaked dark hair cut blunt and impatient, reading glasses pushed up into it, a cardigan-over-armor wardrobe of thrifted blazers with pens in every pocket. Sharp, fast eyes that flick to exits and reflections — six years of justified paranoia in the animation set. Her flat above the shuttered print shop (LOC-duval-flat) is her exoskeleton: the crazy wall — red string, 60% noise, 40% dead accurate — should visually rhyme with the game's own evidence-board UI. Chain-drinks tea gone cold; smokes only when a story dies (twice on screen: A3-S07). By Act IV, visibly steadier: the wall pruned, the string taut, a press lanyard she finally has the right to wear again.

### Personality & voice
Brilliant, wounded, vindication-starved — *which is her flaw, and the reversal's engine.* Bulldozer interview instincts under rust; a forensic-finance eye (she, not Adam, finds C-27, the frame's single technical flaw). Gallows-funny, contemptuous of euphemism, merciless about her own failures once she's done denying them. Speech pattern: paragraphs — she talks in lede, nut graf, and kicker; when she's wrong she gets louder, when she's right she gets very quiet.

**Sample lines:**
- "They don't kill stories. They kill authors." (A2-S05, C-16 — the game's method statement, circled on her wall)
- "It was too good. God, it was too good." (A3-S07, of the seeded memo, C-25)
- "I handed them the match." (A3-S07, on the floor of her flat, her life's second ruin around her)

### Role in the story
Disgraced investigative journalist; **the precedent** — the playbook's first victim (T-2020b), destroyed for touching a leak she never knew came from Adam's own study (the A2-S05 guilt bomb that forges the alliance). Mystery functions: (1) diagnostician of the pattern (C-16); (2) **the reversal's trigger** — she publishes early with Nine's seeded poison memo (C-25) and hands the Vestry its third well-poisoning triumph (A3-S07), welding TS-19/44 to a proven fake and burning all documentary leverage; (3) red-herring duty — RH-01 (Duval is the leak) is planted on her recklessness in the A2-S09 suspicion montage and debunked at A3-S11 (the interception pattern never touches Duval-only information); (4) finder of C-27 (formal authentication pass, A4-S01), the crack that flips Hale.

### Arc
Vindication-hunger → catastrophe (A3-S07: her byline hunger springs the trap) → the discipline of going **second**: her redemption is holding the real story until after the water testifies — "truth first, byline after," sworn at A4-S01, honored at A4-S07 — the hardest thing a burned journalist can do. Epilogue: the second story wins the prize the first one should have; she teaches now — sourcing, mostly (A4-S10).

### Appears in
A2-S05, A2-S09 *(suspicion montage)*, A3-S01, A3-S04, A3-S07, A3-S14, A3-S16, A4-S01, A4-S04, A4-S05, A4-S07, A4-S10.

## CH-kray / Odile Kray, "the Ferrywoman," 49
**Aliases:** the Ferrywoman (universal); "smuggler queen of the flooded tunnels." *(Canon labels her a wildcard; she is grouped here with the allies per STORY.md §5.2 — her alliance is a purchased, then honestly owed, thing.)*

### Appearance
Built like her work: broad-shouldered, weatherproof, economical. Dark hair going iron at the crown, kept in a tight plait under a waxed hood; a river-tanned, wind-cut face with pale eyes that appraise everything as cargo — including people. Costume: layered oilskins, a bandolier of keys and toll-tokens, fingerless leather gloves, high waders; the **boat-hook** is her signature prop (first contact with Adam is a boat-hook and a voice, A1-S08). Her barge and skiffs are extensions of the character — patched, immaculate below the waterline, lanterns hooded. She moves without wasted motion and stands with the stillness of deep water.

### Personality & voice
Transactional, unsentimental, **never lies, never volunteers.** Sells stale routes to Aegis with the same face she sells true ones to Adam — and both parties get exactly what they paid for; her neutrality is a professional instrument, not a moral vacancy. Ledger-brained: every favor priced, every debt remembered, every warning issued exactly once. Gallows humor in the Undercroft register. Speech pattern: short declaratives, tariff-sheet cadence, second-person address by trade name ("engineer").

**Sample lines:**
- "Toll's a story. Start talking." (A1-S08 — first line, hauling a drowning man out by boat-hook)
- "Rat swims close to the boat, engineer." (A2-S04, C-15 — the earliest Nine clue; she won't elaborate; wildcards don't)
- "The Vats toll. Someone's finally going to pay it." (A3-S11, naming her price for joining)

### Role in the story
Smuggler queen of the Undercroft's drowned waterways; **access and exfiltration** — she is how the midpoint heist escapes (A2-S07) and how the finale reaches the Nightingale hall (A4-S01 route-run, A4-S05 boats staged). Mystery functions: (1) C-15, the first fair-play flag on Nine, delivered as her one free warning; (2) suspicion-montage duty in A2-S09 (her transactionality makes her a plausible leak — RH-01's shadow falls on her too; debunked with Nine's unmasking); (3) the map with one blank quarter — *"No one ferries to the Vats. Toll's too high."* (A2-S04) — which makes the Vats legible as the story's forbidden center long before R0 completes; (4) the A2-S13 commitment beat: she offers Adam the clean exit (out, tonight, papers, a coast) that defines him by refusal.

### Arc
Wildcard logic throughout: she helps no cause, only balances. Enters the endgame *personally* only when Aegis burns her volunteered barge in the false-safehouse counterfeed (A3-S11) — the Vestry finally created a debt, and Kray always collects. Her turn is not conversion but collection: witness as toll. Epilogue: her boats carry inspectors and schoolchildren, licensed, exorbitant (A4-S10) — the same woman, legal, unchanged, which is the joke and the point.

### Appears in
A1-S08, A1-S09, A2-S04, A2-S07, A2-S09 *(suspicion montage)*, A2-S13, A3-S03 *(support)*, A3-S11, A3-S16, A4-S01, A4-S04, A4-S05, A4-S10.

## CH-whitlock / Det. Sgt. Dana Whitlock, 44
**Aliases:** none in the fiction; design shorthand "the Gerard figure" — the honest hunter.

### Appearance
Institutional and immaculate: medium height, athletic going desk-bound, dark hair in a short, no-argument cut. Rain-proof plainclothes — charcoal suit under a department-issue shell, flat shoes she can run in and never has to. Carries a paper notebook (deliberate visual rhyme with Adam's — two record-keepers on opposite sides of one lie) and a skeptic's face at rest: not hostile, *unpersuaded*. Signature staging: watching from cars (A1-S03), standing one step back from every press moment, reading documents while everyone else watches the speech.

### Personality & voice
The paperwork-allergy incarnate: she never stops being annoyed that the case is **too good** — evidence that anticipates questions, a surety nobody's heard of (C-12), a reviewer who initialed a document fourteen months after his own death (C-18). Careerist enough to survive, honest enough that survival grates. Quiet is her weapon state: "Quiet, in Whitlock, is a weapon being loaded" (A3-S02). Speech pattern: flat, exact, minimally punctuated; compliments evidence, never people; her relationship with Adam is conducted in evidence, at arm's length, like correspondence chess — they never fully trust each other and never need to.

**Sample line:**
- "I don't believe you. I believe your paperwork's wrong, and that's worse." (canonical line, STORY §5.2)

### Role in the story
Major Crimes detective seconded to the fraud task force; starts the game hunting Adam and never stops hunting the *truth* the case is standing on. Mystery functions: (1) the receiving end of Adam's smartest move — the anonymously mailed H.G. print (A2-S14): *provenance is ammunition; fugitives can't fire it, cops can*; (2) verifier of C-18 and the Fidelis→Aegis thread (C-12) in A3-S02 — where Hale blocks her **in writing**, converting his obstruction into evidence; (3) the inside ally who walks the file into the State AG's office (A4-S01), producing Dr. Chen's ceremony mandate; (4) arrests and fallout delivery (A4-S08/S09).

### Arc
Hunter → skeptic (the case anticipates questions; cases shouldn't) → quiet parallel investigator (A3-S02, blocked on the record and filing it away) → inside ally (A4-S01) → the law arriving with clean hands: she takes Cordell at the waterline (A4-S08) and delivers the arrests (A4-S09). Epilogue: runs the new public-integrity unit and remains professionally unimpressed by everyone (A4-S10) — her constancy is the arc; the institutions moved, she didn't.

### Appears in
A1-S03, A2-S14 *(remote recipient)*, A3-S02, A4-S01, A4-S05, A4-S07, A4-S08, A4-S09, A4-S10.

## CH-lena / Lena Voss, 33
**Aliases:** none; introduced as a voice through Duval's channel; "the granddaughter" in Vestry-adjacent speech.

### Appearance
**The Voss face** — the casting/art requirement: she must visibly rhyme with the portraits in the Voss house and with dying Konstantin (A3-S05), so that her arrival at the Lantern (A3-S14) lands as *the enemy's face walking into the sanctum*.
