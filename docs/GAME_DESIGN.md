# Purple Rain — Game Design Brief (v0.1)

> Initial design brief. Everything here is a starting point for iteration, not a locked spec.

## Story Summary

The protagonist is an ordinary, competent professional whose life is quietly perfect — until he is framed as the linchpin of a large-scale conspiracy he knew nothing about. In a matter of days he loses everything: his job, his home, his savings, his reputation, and the people who trusted him. The system that should protect him is the same system the conspirators control.

With nothing left to lose, he goes underground. To reclaim his life he must become someone the old him would never recognize — investigating the conspiracy from its edges to its center, breaking the law when the law is a weapon pointed at him, solving the puzzles and riddles the conspirators use to hide the truth, and walking through a figurative *heaven and hell*: the paradise he lost, the underworld he must survive, and the long climb back. The endgame: clear his name, take his revenge, and stop the conspirators before their plan reaches its final stage.

## Gameplay Pillars

1. **First-person action** — grounded first-person movement, evasion, and confrontation. Tense, physical, personal.
2. **Investigation** — evidence gathering, surveillance, document trails, interrogating leads; the player assembles the conspiracy piece by piece.
3. **Puzzles & riddles** — environmental puzzles, ciphers, and riddles guard the truth; the conspirators speak in symbols.
4. **Narrative quests** — a quest-driven structure where story beats, choices, and consequences carry the experience.

## Core Loop

```
INVESTIGATE  →  ACT (heist / infiltrate / confront)  →  PUZZLE  →  CONSEQUENCE / REVEAL
     ↑                                                                    │
     └────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

- **Investigate:** pick up a lead — a name, a location, a document. Surveil, eavesdrop, search.
- **Act:** use what you learned to get somewhere or something you shouldn't — break in, infiltrate, steal, tail, confront. First-person action lives here.
- **Puzzle:** the prize is locked — a cipher, a riddle, a mechanism, a hidden meaning. Solve it.
- **Consequence / Reveal:** the truth advances, but so does the cost — heat rises, allies waver, the conspiracy adapts. New leads open; the loop restarts one rung deeper (or higher).

## Act Structure — the Heaven / Hell Arc

| Act | Arc stage | Content sketch |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Act 0 — Grace** | The life he had | Playable prologue in the protagonist's intact life. Tutorializes movement/interaction. Ends with the frame-up landing all at once. |
| **Act I — The Fall** | Fall from grace | Everything is stripped away in real time. On the run, learning the loop: first investigations, first crimes of necessity, first riddle from the conspirators. |
| **Act II — Hell** | Descent | Rock bottom: the criminal underworld, the people the system already discarded. The darkest jobs, the hardest moral compromises, the deepest layer of the conspiracy. He becomes capable — and risks becoming what they said he was. |
| **Act III — Purgatory** | The climb | Turning evidence into leverage. Rebuilding alliances, choosing what kind of man climbs back out. Taking the fight from the shadows toward the conspirators' own ground. |
| **Act IV — Reckoning** | Judgment | Clearing his name publicly, revenge on the men who framed him, and stopping the conspiracy's endgame. What "heaven" means now is defined by the player's accumulated choices. |

## Tone & References

- **Tone:** grounded neo-noir thriller with mythic/religious undertones (the heaven–hell framing is figurative, not literal fantasy). Rain-soaked, purple-and-neon night palette. Melancholy but propulsive — a man with nothing left is dangerous.
- **Reference points (to interrogate, not copy):**
  - *The Fugitive* / *The Count of Monte Cristo* — the framed-man revenge spine.
  - *Dishonored* — fall-from-grace structure, player-defined morality.
  - *Deus Ex* — first-person investigation + infiltration in a conspiracy plot.
  - *Firewatch / Gone Home* — first-person environmental storytelling.
  - *The Witness / Outer Wilds* — puzzles as revelation rather than obstacle.
  - Dante's *Inferno* — the descent/climb structure, named-circle chapter framing.

## Open Questions

- **Combat depth:** full combat system, or evasion/stealth-first with scarce, high-stakes violence?
- **Structure:** linear act progression, hub-based (safehouse + districts), or semi-open city?
- **Morality system:** do crimes-of-necessity mechanically track and change the ending, or is consequence purely narrative?
- **Riddle authorship:** are the conspirators' riddles diegetic (an in-world cabal aesthetic) or a gameplay convention?
- **The "heaven" ending:** can the protagonist actually get his old life back, or only something new? Multiple endings?
- **Scope:** target length (8–12h narrative game vs. 20h+ with side quests)? Team size and content budget will decide.
- **Name:** how literally does the title *Purple Rain* tie into the fiction (a signal, a codename, a storm, a song)?
